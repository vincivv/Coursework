## Purpose

The goal of this milestone is to **present, explain, and defend** the database system you built throughout the semester.

This is not a new technical milestone and it is not a coding exercise. Instead, this is your opportunity to step back and explain:

- what you built,
- why you built it the way you did,
- what tradeoffs you faced,
- and how all the pieces fit together.

This milestone mirrors how engineers present systems to technical audiences in the real software industry.

---

## Presentation Overview

Each team will deliver a **short, focused presentation** covering the full lifecycle of their project.

### Time Limits

- **8–10 minutes** for the presentation  
- **1 minute** for questions from the audience  

Going significantly over time will impact your evaluation.

---

## What to Present

Your presentation should focus on **architectural reasoning and ownership**, not on slides full of screenshots or code.

You are expected to cover, at a high level:

### 1. Project Overview

- What problem does your database system solve?
- What makes your project unique compared to existing solutions?

### 2. Database Design and Architecture

- Key design decisions from Milestones 1 and 2
- Normalization choices and tradeoffs
- Schema structure and relationships

### 3. Business Rules and Database Logic

- Examples of important business rules implemented in Milestone 3
- Why these rules belong in the database
- Challenges encountered when implementing them

### 4. ORM and API Design and Usage

- How your ORM is structured
- How models and services abstract SQL
- Key challenges faced when designing a domain-specific ORM

### 5. Live Demonstration

You must include a **short live demonstration** of your ORM in action.

This can be:
- a simple command-line interface, or
- a minimal local web page (e.g., a single HTML page on localhost).

The demo should show:
- model creation,
- service method usage,
- and database interaction without writing raw SQL.

The demo should be short and reliable. Do not over-engineer it.

---

## Evaluation and Ownership

Grading for this milestone happens **during the presentation**.

You will be evaluated on:

- clarity of explanation,
- understanding of architectural decisions,
- ability to justify tradeoffs,
- coherence of the system as a whole,
- and demonstrated ownership of the work.

There is no partial credit for slides or demos alone. If team members cannot explain the system they present, the 
presentation will not receive a passing evaluation.

---


## Final Notes

This milestone is not about perfection. It is about **understanding**.

Strong presentations show that you can reason about systems, explain your decisions, and communicate technical 
ideas clearly. Those skills matter far beyond this course.

Treat this presentation as you would a technical walkthrough in a real engineering team: concise, honest, and 
grounded in the work you actually did.
