## Milestones Breakdown

This repository is structured around five milestones. Together, they contribute a total of **70 points** toward your final grade.

Extra credit (3 points) is awarded to the team with the best milestone submission.  
Extra credit is awarded only to team members who regularly contributed and demonstrated ownership during team meetings.

In this class, who submits the work is less important than who takes ownership of it. Ownership is evaluated during in-class team meetings, where the instructor will challenge design and implementation decisions and students must defend their choices. This mirrors how engineering teams are evaluated in the real software industry, especially in the AI era.

## Submission and Grading Policies

This section defines non-negotiable submission and grading rules that apply to all milestones.

Each milestone has its own README file with specific submission and grading criteria. These instructions must be followed precisely.

General policies:

- All work must be committed to the `master` branch of your assigned repository. Only the `master` branch will be graded.
- Individual contributions are evaluated based on commits and demonstrated ownership. By committing work, you assert that you fully understand it and can defend it during team meetings. If you cannot do so, the work will not be considered yours and will be graded accordingly.
- Due dates are final. Plan ahead.
- The team lead is fully responsible for milestone submission. Submitting a blank file or broken link results in a zero.
- For each milestone version, teams must submit or update a `contributions.md` file documenting each member’s contributions.
- Late submissions are not accepted. Each milestone has two versions: Version 1 for feedback and Version 2 for final grading.

For full course policies, refer to the course syllabus in Canvas.

## Grade Appeal Policy

You may request a grade revision **within one week** of the grade being posted on Canvas.

To appeal:
1. Schedule office hours with the instructor.
2. Clearly identify where points were deducted in error.

After one week, all grades are final.
